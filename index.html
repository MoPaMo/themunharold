2023 annual MUN conference takes place in Berlin

December 7, 2023 by Moritz

More than 100 delegates from countries all over the world met in four committees to stand up for their country. TheMUN Harold was there for you and reports on the most important events from the PC.

The 2023 MUN conference was held in the catholic Theresa school in Berlin today. Bright minds from 24 different countries came together to shape the future of the world, at least in theory. As always, delegations were sent to four different committees, the Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC), the Human Rights Committee (HRC), the Political Committee (PC), and the Disarmament Committee (DC). The entire conference was centered around the question "Building a new future - How can we shape the future while also solving the problems of the present?".
Our journalists were able to report from the PC, which discussed the topic “Safeguarding privacy and preventing censorship in the digital age”.
Our reporters noticed that while initially only two major groups formed during lobbying, one of which included India, Bhutan, China, Russia, and Afghanistan among others, the groups were forced to split up into three by the chairs after about an hour. This was met with discontent and incomprehension by the delegates.
Also, this meant that the newly created group, meant to be neutral, was largely influenced by the Russia-China con group, as many of its members originated from that group.
Nevertheless, the con group struggled to get its opinion through. The Chinese delegate also voiced his thoughts on why that could be.
In the end, both the resolution from the con group, with China as its main- and Russia, Afghanistan, North Korea, and Iran as its co-submitters, and the one from the pro group, with the US as the main submitter and support from Germany, Japan, Ireland, France, and others. The neutral resolution did not pass.
However, it seemed to our reporters that the pro group was more successful than the con or neutral group, as they were able to amend many clauses to the resolutions that were passed.

China accuses MUN of biased country list

December 7, 2023 by Moritz

Discontent grew in the Chinese delegation as the country list was published. The PRC accused the TheMUN team of selecting too many Western or pro-Western countries for the conference. TheMUN Harold reached out to the Chinese delegation for a statement.

The Chinese delegate said: "We were very disappointed to learn that that no other countries from Southeast Asia, one of the most densely populated areas in the world and also a rapidly emerging market, were selected for the conference. This left us with no allies in the region and we feel that this [...] is a clear case of bias." The delegate also voiced his dissatisfaction with the disproportionately low number of African states selected: "This too has deprived us of many allies."
However, the delegate acknowledged that this was also a special case in his committee, where up to ten countries were not represented at all.

While it's clear that some regions are heavily over-represented (where the ratio of population to country is extremely low), the Harold team would also like to point out that population representation alone does not necessarily make for a good list; geopolitical influence should also be taken into account. The Chinese delegation did not wish to comment on this.

"Banners row" sees Israel hit hard

December 8, 2023 by Moritz

The dispute over banners in the PC escalated today when the Israeli delegate repeatedly held up banners with the countries flag and the words "Free Israel" on it. The delegate was asked to stop by the chairs, but refused to do so. He was noted for punishment.
The delegate continued to display the flag, although not as high as before. Eventually, administrators were asked by the chairs to take away the delegate's banners. The delegate refused to give them up and the admins had to take the banners by force. This included ripping paper out of the reader the delegate had drawn the banner on.

The delegate received at least ten calls for order and punishments. Nevtheless, due to time constraints, the delegate was only published three times. This included singing "Let It Go" from Frozen and "Last Christmas" by Wham!. The delegate was also forced to dance a Let's Dance choreography to the song "Rasputin" by Boney M.

"He didn't have respect for other delegates" one of the chairs told our newspaper, "Political Messages in any form are prohibited, thats all", the other said.

The TheMUN Harold also asked the delegate for a statement, but he did not respond in time.
